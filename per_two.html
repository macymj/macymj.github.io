While at school, film students should be sure to pay attention to their classes first and foremost. Students should take courses that will expand their mind, their craft, and their reel. I try to be on the lookout in particular courses that teach me new skills or strengthen my weak spots.
However, it should be noted that anyone can learn without classes, if need be. Online resources, practice, and exploration can teach a whole lot if you're dedicated enough. Personally, I taught myself to use Adobe Photoshop and it is still the program I know best.
I have found it ideal to build a schedule that gives me a good variety of skills and understandings. To do so, I double-majored in Communications with a concentration in Media Production and in Media and Journalism with a concentration in Advertising. I also minored in Global Cinema. Combined, my studies teach me all about creative production in both art and business, and film theory. I also take courses in writing, website coding, and public relations.
Along with classes, I believe you should get involved in campus groups. Especially at larger colleges, there are often groups dedicated to student creatives. At Carolina, Carolina Film Association and Student Television offer media makers a space and resources to start producing content they can add to their reel. Getting involved with other creative endeavors will also expand your resume and your mind. I worked at Playmakers making props for professional and student theater productions.
I also explored the organizational side of film by becoming the Junior Representative for Swain Lot Film Festival. Swain Lot is a student-run celebration of student film created here at UNC.
As you can see, there are plenty of options for learning opportunities at school, wherever you are. You just have to do your best to keep an open mind and busy schedule.
